Key Takeaways for Indian Companies

1
DPDPA enforcement hasn't started yet — but it will
The Data Protection Board of India is not yet constituted. When it is, the ₹250 crore per violation penalty will be live. Start compliance now, not when the Board is operational.
2
CERT-In's 6-hour rule is active and enforced now
Unlike DPDPA, CERT-In's breach reporting mandate is in force since June 2022. Failure to report within 6 hours carries criminal penalty. Automate breach detection.
3
Data localisation is India's most effective enforcement tool
RBI's business bans on Mastercard and Amex show India will use operational bans, not just fines. DPDPA is expected to include data localisation requirements for sensitive data.
4
Healthcare & Fintech face dual regulatory pressure
Star Health (IRDAI + CERT-In) and payment firms (RBI + DPDPA) face obligations from multiple regulators simultaneously. A single platform covering all is essential.
